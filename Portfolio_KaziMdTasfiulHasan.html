About Me

I am a passionate and self-motivated 12th grade student from Bangladesh with strong interests in Civil Engineering and Architecture. Despite my age, I have independently learned to design 2D and 3D building plans using AutoCAD and SketchUp. My designs have been used in real-life family construction projects, including a 4-story engineer-approved residential building on my grandfather’s land, and a 6-story family-owned structure — of which 3 floors have already been constructed.

I aspire to study Civil Engineering or Architecture in Japan, inspired by the country’s innovation, sustainability, and engineering excellence. I deeply admire Japan’s earthquake-resilient design, smart city planning, and harmonious balance between tradition and technology. This curiosity has driven me to begin learning the Japanese language (currently studying for JLPT N5), in hopes of better understanding both the culture and academic environment.

My long-term goal is to return to Bangladesh and contribute to national development. As my country is still growing in terms of infrastructure, I want to use the advanced skills and ideas I gain in Japan to bring innovation to construction, improve safety and efficiency, and help shape a better future for the people of Bangladesh. I am dedicated to making a meaningful impact by applying cutting-edge knowledge and sustainable practices to help my country thrive.
